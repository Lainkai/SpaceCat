Space Cat

What is Space Cat?

Space Cat is a mnemonic acronym for A.P. Lang Students to help them discover the composition of a piece of writing. These questions can help students become better readers and writers.

Speaker

Asking youself who the speaker is can tell you a lot about the writing. The speaker can give you insight into what kind of bias the piece has. The speaker can be the author, but it can also be a character inside the book. For example, in Lou Gehrig's speech, he would be the speaker, while in Harper Lee's To Kill a Mockingbird, Scout Finch is the speaker.

The speaker is Atticus Finch, the charismatic lawyer who is Scouts father. He is known for his wisdom, intelligence, and behavior, and everyone in Maycomb respects him.

"It matters not what someone is born, but what they grow to be." J.K. Rowling

The speaker is Albus Dumbledore, the great wizard of the Harry Potter series. He doesn't judge books by their cover, and welcomes all.

Purpose

What is the author trying to do? I don't know! That is what you're supposed to figure out. There are, but not limited to, three main points:

  • To Persuade

    The author is trying to get the audience to believe or do something. Advertisements easily fit into this category, for they are designed to entice customers, voters, or whatever group the author is trying to target.

  • To Inform

    They want to share you some juicy secrets! The author is going to use facts and information to help the audience gain information about a subject.

  • To Entertain

    Funny Joke! The author is trying to entertain the audience. The author can employ a variety of techniques to do so, but they are not limited to jokes. The author could be recalling on his/her childhood, or he/she could be trying to fright his/her way into joy.

The author is not limited to these three examples, but that covers the gist.

Do not confuse the purpose with rhetorical appeals!

Audience

Who is the author writing to?

Children?

Doctor Seuss is famous for his childrens books, but would you want to read an entire romantic novel in rhymes? I hope not.

Young Adults?

Young adults different needs compared to children, so authors have to write in a diffferent rhetoric in order to appeal to this audience.

JoJo's Bizzare Adventure includes many references that young children may not understand.

Context

What's up with the world? No. Seriously, what is going on with the world when the author was writing this? Think about the time period, political affiliations, wars, and global events.

Example

Fahrenheit 451 by Ray Bradbury was written in 1953. This is during the peak of McCarthyism, where anyone who dissented upon the U.S. government was considered to be a 'communist' and ridiculed by society.

Exigence

Why would someone in their right mind would write an essay about ugly tourists? Clearly something had to make the author mad or something.

Exigence is the reason why the author created the peace. Current events are common forms of exigence for newspapers.

Choices

Why did the author choose this word to be here? What kind of rhetorical device did the author use in this sentence?

These are specific choices that the author made. Word choice, metaphors, or sentence structure are all forms of choices the authors can make.
